Swimming Instructor

Sep 2017 - Mar 2018

Chaco Swim

In the summer of 2017, I had been doing competitive swimming for a few years. My club also taught swimming lessons after our practices, so I decided to help out and volunteer.

For the first week I followed an instructor, and the next week, I was assigned, my own class. For the first session, I taught a group of beginners, consisting of 6 children aged 4-8. I was given instructions and material to introduce but I was free to decide the lessons. It was also my responsibility to ensure the children’s safety, adjust for their learning speeds, and help them if they were upset or scared. After 3 months the session ended and I taught a new class.

JA company program

Oct 2017 - May 2018

Shareholder and Vice President of Marketing

From 2017 to 2018 I was in the Junior Achievement (JA) Company program. Together with other high-school students (the division in Scarborough, sponsored by TD) and 3 professional advisors, we created and ran our own company.

My activities:

  • Decided to sell hoodies after coming up with ideas for products
  • Chose the president and vice presidents of the company through a vote
  • Collected a 25$ investment from each member for a company share
  • Created a company name, logo, and mission statement
  • Planned out the year and scheduled regular company meetings
  • Assigned individual jobs or worked together to promote the company, buy materials, find customers, produce and deliver products
  • Created a website to promote our product
  • Managed the company’s social media accounts
  • Created a video commercial for the JA “battle of the ads”
  • I attended a JA tradeshow with 2 others and set up a stand to promote and sell our products
  • Wrote a shareholders report summarizing what we did each month

Throughout our operations, we faced many challenges and disadvantages. Our company only had 13 members, which was less than most groups, so we had to be efficient and make the most out of our time. The market for clothing was very competitive, and most people would rather buy hoodies from more popular brands. In order to stand out, we named our company “Inclusion” and sold hoodies with positive messages on them, that one could wear to show support to minority groups or stand up to bullying. We also increased the demand for our products by selling online through facebook, Instagram, and our own company website.

Although we were initially unsuccessful, we eventually managed to make a profit, even after paying the $250 dollar tax to JA. We did not earn enough to win any awards, but I gained valuable experience in all areas of business and running a company.
